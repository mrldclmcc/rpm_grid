Baby Groot Program Viewer

Program Philosophy: The Novice's Path

The "Baby Groot" program is a 12-week hypertrophy routine designed by John Meadows specifically for new lifters (with 1 year or less of experience). The goal is to build a solid foundation by focusing on frequency, progressive overload, and proper mind-muscle connection.

Unlike many programs that train a muscle once a week, this split hits each muscle group 2-3 times per week to maximize growth. The key is to train hard but smart. Meadows believes that training a muscle every 2-3 days is optimal to stimulate muscle protein synthesis and avoid losing out on gains.

The 12-Week Progression

The program follows a 3-day split, alternating weekly between a primary "A" set of workouts and a "B" set. This ensures you are constantly challenging your muscles from different angles and with different movements.

  • Weeks 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 11: Perform workouts "Upper A," "Lower A," "Full A".
  • Weeks 2, 4, 6, 8, 10, 12: Perform workouts "Upper B," "Lower B," "Full B".

Progression Goals

This is the most important part of the program. You must strive to get stronger or do more reps over time. Do not just go through the motions.

  • Compound Lifts: Aim to add 30-60 lbs to your starting weights (e.g., Squat, Deadlift, Bench Press) by the end of 12 weeks.
  • Isolation Lifts: Aim to add 6 additional reps to your starting rep range (e.g., Curls, Tricep Extensions) by the end of 12 weeks.

Understanding RPE

RPE stands for **Rate of Perceived Exertion**. It's a way to measure how hard you're working. Meadows designed this program with RPE in mind to prevent overtraining and ensure you're lifting with intent. For a beginner, it is not necessary to go to failure.

RPE 8: You could do 2-3 more reps. This is the sweet spot for most sets, stimulating growth without burning you out. Meadows recommends an RPE of 8 for your compound lifts.

RPE 8.5: You have 2 more reps left in the tank. Meadows recommends this RPE for isolation exercises, as they tend to allow for less form breakdown and are thus safer to push a little harder.

Diet, Nutrition, and Rest

For optimal results and recovery, it's crucial to focus on diet and rest. The program emphasizes the concept of Net Protein Balance, which is a scale between Muscle Protein Synthesis (building muscle) and Muscle Protein Breakdown (breaking down muscle).

  • Diet and Nutrition: To stimulate Muscle Protein Synthesis, consume a diet with adequate calories and protein. Eating enough carbohydrates can help manage Muscle Protein Breakdown. The goal is to maintain a positive net protein balance.
  • Rest and Recovery: You must be recovered to train effectively. The program suggests training a muscle group 2-3 times per week, as Muscle Protein Synthesis is stimulated for about 48 hours after a workout. If you are still feeling sore, your muscle fibers have not fully recovered, and you should not train that muscle group to avoid overtraining. The program is designed with a lower number of sets and moderate intensity to help maximize this recovery.
